Case Study: Climate Action Plan

Recap of case study claim - Humans get energy from sources that release far too much carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases.

Because humans release so much carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases into the atmosphere, ocean temperatures are rapidly rising. This leads to glaciers melting, which results in rising sea levels, and temperature increases change habitats for marine life, which can lead to hazards for humans.
Effectiveness claim - Effective ways to reduce human impact would be changing our sources of energy.

By using different sources of energy (taking advantage of marine winds) and removing carbon dioxide in the oceans and atmosphere, we can reduce the effects of global warming and climate change.
Use of tech claim - We can use wind turbines as a more sustainable source of energy that doesn’t contribute to climate change.

By taking advantage of alternative energy sources such as wind turbines and reducing climate change by storing carbon, we can reduce human harm to the oceans. As a result, we can enhance the resilience of ocean ecosystems that provide food, jobs, recreational opportunities, cultural identity, and more.
Implications for my actual project - In order to reduce the drastic change in oceans created by man-made climate change, we should spread awareness and take measures to restrict society from contributing to climate change.

Through spreading awareness, we can get people to take action through choice. Society needs to change to reduce climate change, and if people know what’s going on, they’ll be more willing to accept the changes. The measures taken can mostly impact larger corporations and won’t affect individuals much, and through this we can take care of the largest contributors to climate change.
